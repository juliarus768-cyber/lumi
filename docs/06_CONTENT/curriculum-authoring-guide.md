## Math Adventure 18 – Curriculum Authoring Guide

Version 1.0 Guide for Curriculum Writers & AI Content Creation

## Purpose

This guide establishes the standards for creating educational content in Math Adventure. Every question, hint, explanation, and Tutor Mode script should follow these guidelines to ensure a consistent, engaging, and pedagogically sound learning experience.

## 1. Core Principles

- Teach understanding before memorization.

- Write for confidence, not intimidation.

- Every mistake is a learning opportunity.

- Questions should feel like adventures, not worksheets.

- Use clear, age-appropriate language.

## 2. Writing Learning Objectives

- Define one measurable objective per skill.

- Identify prerequisite knowledge.

- Describe the expected evidence of mastery.

- Connect the objective to the Ontario curriculum.

## 3. Question Design Standards

- Focus on a single concept whenever possible.

- Avoid unnecessary reading complexity.

- Use real-world contexts children recognize.

- Provide a clear correct answer.

- Tag every question with curriculum metadata.

## 4. Difficulty Levels

| Level | Goal | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Build confidence | Simple recall |
| 2 | Practice | Single-step application |
| 3 | Apply | Mixed practice |
| 4 | Reason | Multi-step problems |
| 5 | Extend | Challenge/enrichment |

## 5. Tutor Mode Content

- Start with encouragement.

- Ask a guiding question before giving a hint.

- Provide hints from general to specific.

- Explain the concept in simple language.

- Offer a similar practice opportunity later.

## 6. Hint Writing

- Hint 1: Prompt thinking.

- Hint 2: Narrow the focus.

- Hint 3: Demonstrate the next step without solving everything.

## 7. Explanation Guidelines

- Keep explanations under 100 words when possible.

- Use examples and visuals where appropriate.

- Avoid mathematical jargon unless introduced first.

- Celebrate learning, not correctness.

## 8. Word Problems

- Use realistic family, school, shopping, sports and hobby contexts.

- Avoid cultural bias.

- Keep numbers appropriate for the target grade.

- Ensure reading level matches the intended age.

## 9. Metadata Requirements

- Grade

- Strand

- Topic

- Skill

- Difficulty

- Misconception tags

- Estimated solving time

- Tutor script reference

## 10. AI Content Review

- AI-generated questions require validation.

- Verify curriculum alignment.

- Check numerical accuracy.

- Review reading level.

- Reject repetitive or ambiguous wording.

## 11. Content Quality Checklist

- Learning objective is clear.

- Question has one best answer.

- Hints are progressive.

- Explanation is encouraging.

- Metadata is complete.

- Question supports adaptive learning.

## 12. Future Expansion

- Reading Adventure authoring standards.

- Science experiments and inquiry prompts.

- Financial literacy scenarios.

- Teacher-created custom content.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Every new question follows this guide.

- AI-generated content matches the same standards as human-authored content.

- Curriculum quality remains consistent as the question bank grows.
