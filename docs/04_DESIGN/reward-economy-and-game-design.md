## Math Adventure Reward Economy & Game Design

Version 1.0 Progression, Motivation & Engagement Blueprint

## Purpose

This document defines how Math Adventure motivates children through meaningful progression, rewards, collectibles, and family experiences while reinforcing learning rather than replacing it.

## Core Design Principles

- Learning is always the primary reward.

- Rewards reinforce consistency, not perfection.

- Family experiences are more valuable than money.

- Children should save toward long-term goals.

- No pay-to-win mechanics.

- Rewards should encourage intrinsic motivation over time.

## Progression Systems

- Knowledge XP: earned for mastering concepts.

- Courage XP: earned for persistence, using Tutor Mode, and correcting mistakes.

- Coins: earned for completing adventures.

- Badges: earned for milestones and habits.

- Adventure Map: visual long-term progression.

## Daily Rewards

- Complete Daily Adventure: Coins + XP

- Perfect effort (not perfect score): Bonus Courage XP

- Bonus Mission: Additional Coins and XP

- Challenge Mission: Rare badge or collectible

## Weekly Rewards

- Complete all weekday missions to unlock Weekend Pass.

- Weekend Pass allows skipping weekend math while preserving the streak.

- Family reward unlocked: Friday Takeout Dinner (if all weekday missions completed).

- Weekly Mystery Chest.

- Apple Gift Card

- PlayStation Gift Card

## Reward Economy Rules

- Money rewards require long-term saving.

- Minimum money reward is $2.

- Gift cards require multiple weeks of consistent participation.

- Store purchases require parent approval.

- Coins are deducted only after approval.

- Purchase history is permanently stored.

## Adventure Map

Every completed daily adventure moves the child farther along an illustrated world map.

- New locations unlock weekly.

- Each location contains themed decorations, badges or collectibles.

- Major milestones unlock celebration scenes.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Children understand what they are working toward.

- Rewards encourage regular learning.

- Store balances persist across sessions.

- Family rewards promote shared experiences.

- Long-term savings teach delayed gratification.
